Training Program for New Team Members

This section provides structured training materials to help new team members understand FDA requirements for epinephrine auto-injectors and develop the skills needed for successful testing and regulatory submissions.

Learning Objectives

  1. Understand the evolution of FDA guidance for epinephrine auto-injectors from 2016 to 2025
  2. Master the principles and application of Population Bioequivalence (PBE) analysis
  3. Develop proficiency in designing and executing the five key functional tests
  4. Learn to interpret test results and address common testing challenges
  5. Gain skills in preparing comprehensive documentation for regulatory submissions

Training Modules

Module 1: Understanding FDA Requirements (2 Days)

Activity Description Duration
Lecture: Evolution of FDA Guidance Comprehensive overview of how FDA requirements have evolved from 2016 to 2025 3 hours
Workshop: Guidance Document Analysis Small groups analyze different sections of FDA guidance documents and present key findings 4 hours
Case Study: Successful and Failed Submissions Analysis of real-world ANDA submissions, identifying critical success factors and common pitfalls 3 hours

Module 2: Statistical Methods for Bioequivalence (2 Days)

Activity Description Duration
Lecture: PBE Fundamentals Detailed explanation of Population Bioequivalence principles, formulas, and applications 3 hours
Workshop: Sample Size Determination Hands-on exercises calculating appropriate sample sizes for different scenarios 3 hours
Computer Lab: Statistical Analysis Practical session using statistical software (SAS or R) to perform PBE analysis on sample datasets 4 hours

Module 3: Functional Testing Methods (3 Days)

Activity Description Duration
Lecture: Test Method Overview Comprehensive review of the five key functional tests and their scientific basis 3 hours
Lab: Delivered Volume Testing Hands-on practice setting up and conducting delivered volume tests 4 hours
Lab: Needle Length & Integrity Testing Practical session on measuring extended needle length and evaluating needle integrity 4 hours
